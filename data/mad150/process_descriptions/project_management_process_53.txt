The following text is about the project management process. It begins when you make an introduction. After making an introduction, the process is split into 2 parallel branches: getting approval and identifying the approval status. When getting approval is completed, one or more of the following paths should be executed: working out its value, recording the basic idea. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then the process is split into 3 parallel branches: drawing up a project plan, setting a budget and stating the available resources. After setting a budget, laying out a timeline needs to be done. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then having a kickoff meeting should be done. When having a kickoff meeting is completed, the next tasks should be recording KPIs and keeping communication, they should be done in parallel. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then ensuring the project is completed should be done. The process is now completed.

The text below is about the startup due diligence for a venture capitalist. It begins when you learn why startups fail so often. Once learning why startups fail so often occurs, one or more of the following paths should be executed: specificking product rate of growth, specificking product market share. After specificking product rate of growth, one or more of the following paths should be executed: specificking product market size, checking location of startup, specificking product details. After that, one or more of the following paths should be executed: reviewing expertise, reviewing the business plan. When reviewing the business plan is completed, reviewing team chemistry needs to be done. Then, one or more of the following paths should be executed: checking cash flow, checking exit strategy. Afterwards, you need to project the market share. When projecting the market share is completed, you need to check patents. The process is now completed.

The text below is about the accounts receivable process. It begins when you record the buyer's details. When recording the buyer's details is completed, the next tasks should be sending a credit application form to the buyer and running a credit check on the purchasing company, they should be done in parallel. When sending a credit application form to the buyer is completed, the next tasks should be getting approval for post-credit check review and sending the buyer your terms of sale, they should be done in parallel. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then one or more of the following paths should be executed: checking customer payment terms, sending the invoice in your accounting software. Then, one or more of the following paths should be executed: recording your activity digitally, establishing monthly contact with your client, making a second physical copy. Then, one or more of the following paths should be executed: recording the sales on credit of goods, recording the sales on credit of services. The process is now completed.

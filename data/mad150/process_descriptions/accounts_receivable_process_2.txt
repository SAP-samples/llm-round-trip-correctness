The following text is about the accounts receivable process. It begins when you record the buyer's details. When recording the buyer's details is completed, the process is split into 3 parallel branches: getting approval for post-credit check review, sending a credit application form to the buyer and sending the buyer your terms of sale. After sending the buyer your terms of sale, running a credit check on the purchasing company should be done. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then the next tasks should be generating the invoice, sending the invoice in your accounting software and checking customer payment terms, they should be done in parallel. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then one or more of the following paths should be executed: recording your activity digitally, making a second physical copy. After recording your activity digitally, establishing monthly contact with your client needs to be done. After that, one or more of the following paths should be executed: recording the sales on credit of services, recording the sales on credit of goods. The process is now completed.

The following text is about the startup due diligence for a venture capitalist. It starts with learning why startups fail so often. After learning why startups fail so often, one or more of the following paths should be executed: considering product necessity, specificking product market size. After that, reviewing trustworthiness needs to be done. Once reviewing trustworthiness occurs, one or more of the following paths should be executed: checking cash flow, checking current debt. After that, one or more of the following paths should be executed: analyzing the growth stage of competition, knowing exact competition. Afterwards, checking trademarks should be done. The process is now completed.

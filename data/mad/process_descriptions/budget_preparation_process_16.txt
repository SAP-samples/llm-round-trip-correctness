The following description is about the budget preparation process. It starts with recording basic details. After recording basic details, evaluating any significant variances should be done. After evaluating any significant variances, one or more of the following paths should be executed: prioritizing three to five goals, defining three to five goals. After that, calculating the total annual budget should be done. Once calculating the total annual budget occurs, one or more of the following paths should be executed: projecting total annual income, listing all sources of income. Then, you need to finalize the budget. After finalizing the budget, one or more of the following paths should be executed: assigning management responsibilities, obtaining approval from top management. After assigning management responsibilities, one or more of the following paths should be executed: requesting approval from top management, incorporating into the master budget. The process is now completed.

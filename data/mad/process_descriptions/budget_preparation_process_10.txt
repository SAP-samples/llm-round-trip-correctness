The following description is about the budget preparation process. It starts with setting target date for approval. After setting target date for approval, one or more of the following paths should be executed: evaluating any significant variances, reviewing the most recent budget. After that, the next tasks should be defining three to five goals and determining ways of achieving the goals, they should be done in parallel. Once determining ways of achieving the goals occurs, prioritizing three to five goals needs to be done. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then one or more of the following paths should be executed: calculating the total annual budget, estimating the average monthly cost. Then, you need to project total annual income. After projecting total annual income, one or more of the following paths should be executed: finalizing the budget, recording financial data in a budget spreadsheet. Afterwards, the next tasks should be obtaining approval from top management and requesting approval from top management, they should be done in parallel. When obtaining approval from top management is completed, the next tasks should be assigning management responsibilities and incorporating into the master budget, they should be done in parallel. The process is now completed.

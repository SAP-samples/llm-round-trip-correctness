The following text is about the budget preparation process. It begins when you record basic details. When recording basic details is completed, one or more of the following paths should be executed: reviewing the most recent budget, evaluating any significant variances. After that, one or more of the following paths should be executed: determining ways of achieving the goals, defining three to five goals. After defining three to five goals, you need to prioritize three to five goals. Then, one or more of the following paths should be executed: listing all department expenses, estimating the average monthly cost. After that, projecting total annual income needs to be done. When projecting total annual income is completed, the process is split into 2 parallel branches: recording financial data in a budget spreadsheet and finalizing the budget. Once all pre-tasks are executed, then one or more of the following paths should be executed: requesting approval from top management, obtaining approval from top management. The process is now completed.
